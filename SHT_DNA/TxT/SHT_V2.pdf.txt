5. Accuracy and “any function” caveat
Mathematically, the formulas above are exact under these
conditions:
1.fis in the appropriate class (C 1 orC pw),
2. The levelcis a regular value (no tangential intersec-
tions; i.e.f ′(x)̸= 0 at roots),
3.A (j) andM (j) are differentiable atc, which holds au-
tomatically under ourC 1 and regularity assumptions.
In practice:
·If SHT is known analytically, the root formulas (10)
and (14) give exact symbolic expressions.
·If SHT is known numerically (from data or sampling),
then:
·You approximateA (j)′(c) andM (j)′(c) numerically,

·Substitute into (10)/(14),
·Root estimates converge with the accuracy of your
derivative approximations.
