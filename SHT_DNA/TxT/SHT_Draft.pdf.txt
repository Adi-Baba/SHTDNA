Remarks
(a)Vertical shifts.If you add a constantctof, i.e.gpxq“fpxq`c,
then the minimum shifts fromm f tom f `c, and the SHT is just
shifted in theh-axis. If we decide to always normalise functions
so that minf“0, then SHT becomes strictly injective without
any ambiguity.
(b)Relation to roots and tangents.Once you knowffrom SHT,
you can of course:
•Find roots off´αexactly or numerically,
•Compute tangents at any point viaf 1pxq.
But even without reconstructing the full function, the endpoints
af phq, bf phqalready encode “root-like” separations at each hori-
zontal level.
(c)Beyond convex bowls.For more general (non-convex, multi-
lobed) shapes, you would need a more elaborate version of SHT
(multiple intervals per level, etc.). The idea generalises, but the
bookkeeping is more complex. The theorem above is the clean
core: on a very natural and important classC, SHT is a true
alternative to GMR and is mathematically injective.
