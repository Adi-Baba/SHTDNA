# The Mamton Unit (Mmt)

**Dedication:** This unit is named **Mamton** in loving memory of **Mamta**, representing the "Surface Roughness" or "Skewness" of the DNA atomic distribution.

## Definition
The **Mamton ($\Omega$)** is defined as the magnitude of the Skewness Vector $\mathbf{S}(h)$ of the DNA cross-section. It quantifies the **asymmetry** of the mass distribution, effectively measuring the prominence of the Major and Minor grooves.

## Equation
The Skewness Vector components $S_x$ and $S_y$ are derived from the 3rd-order central moments $\mu_{pq}$:
$$ S_x = \mu_{30} + \mu_{12} $$
$$ S_y = \mu_{03} + \mu_{21} $$

The **Mamton** value is the magnitude:
$$ \Omega(h) = \sqrt{S_x^2 + S_y^2} \quad [Mmt] $$

## Nomenclature
- **Unit Name**: Mamton
- **Abbreviation**: Mmt
- **Symbol**: $\Omega$ (Omega)
- **Dimension**: $Length^3$ ($Å^3$).

## Physical Interpretation
- **Low Mmt ($\approx 70$)**: **Smooth/Regular**. Typical of stiff, uniform structures like **A-tracts**. The grooves are regular and the cross-section is relatively symmetric.
- **High Mmt ($> 3000$)**: **Rough/Asymmetric**. Typical of generic B-DNA with kinks, bends, or frayed ends where the cross-section becomes highly skewed.

## Standard Reference Values
- **A-tracts**: $\sim 70.5$ Mmt (Baseline for regular DNA).
- **Generic B-DNA**: $\sim 300 - 3000+$ Mmt (Highly variable).
