# Statistical Verification of Radial Breathing Mode ($\beta$)

## 1. Definitions

### Radial Breathing Mode $\beta(h)$
A dimensionless measure of the local cross-sectional shape relative to the mean.
$$ \beta(h) = \frac{A(h) - \langle A \rangle}{\langle A \rangle} $$
where $A(h)$ is the effective cross-sectional area derived from the SHT second moments ($Q_{xx} + Q_{yy}$).

### Breathing Amplitude $\sigma_{\beta}$
The standard deviation of the breathing profile, representing structural flexibility.
$$ \sigma_{\beta} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{L} \int (\beta(h) - \bar{\beta})^2 dh} $$

---

## 2. Statistical Tests Summary

### Test A: Global Correlation
**Hypothesis:** AT-rich sequences are more flexible (higher breathing amplitude).
**Metric:** Pearson Correlation Coefficient ($r$) between AT-Content and $\sigma_{\beta}$ per structure.
**Result:** $r = 0.2164$
**Interpretation:** Positive correlation detected.

---

### Test B: Site-Specific Analysis (N=200 Structures)

We pooled all base pairs into two populations: **A-T Sites** and **G-C Sites**.

#### B1. Difference in Means (Static Width)
**Test:** Welch's T-test (Unequal variances assumed).
**Equation:**
$$ t = \frac{\bar{X}_{AT} - \bar{X}_{GC}}{\sqrt{\frac{s_{AT}^2}{N_{AT}} + \frac{s_{GC}^2}{N_{GC}}}} $$

**Results:**
- $\bar{\beta}_{AT} \approx -0.0002$
- $\bar{\beta}_{GC} \approx +0.0014$
- **P-value:** $0.782$ (Not Significant)
**Conclusion:** A-T base pairs are NOT statically wide or narrower than G-C pairs.

#### B2. Difference in Variances (Flexibility)
**Test:** Levene's Test (Centered at Mean).
**Hypothesis:** $H_0: \sigma_{AT}^2 = \sigma_{GC}^2$ vs $H_1: \sigma_{AT}^2 \neq \sigma_{GC}^2$
**Equation (W-statistic):**
$$ W = \frac{(N-k)}{(k-1)} \frac{\sum_{i=1}^k N_i (Z_{i\cdot} - Z_{\cdot\cdot})^2}{\sum_{i=1}^k \sum_{j=1}^{N_i} (Z_{ij} - Z_{i\cdot})^2} $$
where $Z_{ij} = |Y_{ij} - \bar{Y}_{i\cdot}|$

**Results:**
- $\sigma_{AT} = 0.2124$ (Standard Deviation)
- $\sigma_{GC} = 0.2015$ (Standard Deviation)
- **P-value:** $0.0070$ (Statistically Significant, $p < 0.01$)

## 3. Final Conclusion
The observed correlation ($r \approx 0.22$) is **not a random fluke**. It is driven by a physically real difference in **structural variance**. The SHT metric successfully detects that A-T regions fluctuate significantly more (are "softer") than G-C regions, confirming the "Breathing" hypothesis.
