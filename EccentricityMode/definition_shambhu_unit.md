# Shambhian Twist ($\eta_r$)

**Dedication:** This metric is named **Shambhian Twist** in memory of **Shambhu**, representing the measure of cross-sectional deformations in the Shape-Height Transform framework.

## Definition
The **Shambhian Twist ($\eta_r$)** is defined as the eccentricity of the inertial ellipse of the DNA cross-section at height $h$. While mathematically an eccentricity, physically it captures the distinct flattening caused by **Propeller Twist** interactions between base pairs.

## Equation
$$ \eta_r(h) = \sqrt{1 - \frac{\lambda_{2}}{\lambda_{1}}} $$

Where $\lambda_1 \ge \lambda_2$ are the eigenvalues of the second moment matrix $\mathbf{Q}(h)$.

## Nomenclature
- **Unit Name**: Shambhian Twist
- **Symbol**: $\eta_r$ (Eta-sub-r)
- **Dimension**: Dimensionless $[1]$.

## Physical Interpretation
- **$\eta_r \approx 0$**: **Circular**. Low internal deformation.
- **$\eta_r \approx 1$**: **Flat/twisted**. High propeller twist resulting in ribbon-like mass distribution.

## Standard Reference Values
- **B-DNA Baseline**: $\eta_r \approx 0.90 - 0.96$
- **A-tracts**: $\eta_r \approx 0.99$ (High Shambhian Twist).
