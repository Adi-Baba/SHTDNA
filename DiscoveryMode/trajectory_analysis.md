# SHT-Tracking: The Geometric Path of DNA

## Objective
To track the behavior of DNA sequences in the reduced **2D Geometric State Space** defined by the Effective Order Parameters.

## The Parameters from Hell
We identified two effective parameters that govern the mechanics:
1.  **$\Psi_1$ (Integrity Mode)**: Correlated Disorder.
    - $\Psi_1 \sim 0.75 v_0 + 0.56 \Omega$
    - Represents the "Broken" state.
2.  **$\Psi_2$ (Shape Mode)**: The Clean Tuner.
    - $\Psi_2 \sim 0.80 \eta_r$
    - Represents the "Flatness" state.

## Trajectory Analysis
By projecting DNA structures onto this manifold, we observe distinct "Orbital Basins":

### 1. The Rigid Attractor (A-tracts)
- **Coordinates**: High $\Psi_2$ (Flat), Low $\Psi_1$ (Intact).
- **Trajectory**: A-tract sequences "lock" into this basin. The geometry ($\eta_r \to 1$) forces the mechanics ($L_p \to \infty$) and suppresses Roughness ($\Omega \to 0$).
- **State**: The "Crystalline" Phase of DNA.

### 2. The Chaotropic Basin (Generic B-DNA)
- **Coordinates**: Low $\Psi_2$ (Round), Variable $\Psi_1$.
- **Trajectory**: Generic sequences wander stochastically in this basin, exploring high-roughness/high-curvature states.
- **State**: The "Liquid" Phase of DNA.

## Conclusion
DNA mechanics is a transition between two geometric phases:
- **Phase A (Ribbon)**: Order parameter $\Psi_2 \approx 1$. Rigid.
- **Phase B (Coil)**: Order parameter $\Psi_2 < 0.9$. Flexible.

The sequence determines the trajectory by setting the equilibrium value of $\eta_r$.
