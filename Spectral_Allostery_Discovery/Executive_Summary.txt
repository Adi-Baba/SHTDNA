Project: SHT-DNA

Overview
--------
The Matrix-SHT project provides a mathematical framework for DNA structural 
analysis by representing the molecular backbone as a continuous cross-sectional 
mass distribution. By applying the Spherical Harmonics Transform (SHT) to 
observed moment matrices, we have recorded patterns in DNA mechanics with 
significant statisical consistency (noted at 40-sigma).

Technical Parameters & Units
----------------------------
1. Radial Eccentricity (ηr): [Dimensionless]
   Observed Baseline: μ = 0.898. A measure of cross-sectional elongation.
2. Spectral Roughness (Ω): [Angstroms^3 (A^3)]
   A measure of mass asymmetry (skewness), often elevated in protein complexes.
3. Coherence Length (Lc): [Angstroms (A)]
   An indicator of mechanical coupling across the backbone.
4. Spectral Entropy (S): [Nats]
   A measure of the heterogeneity in the longitudinal mass distribution.

Observed Scientific Patterns
----------------------------
1. Mechanical Coupling Observations:
   Our data suggests that protein interactions correlate with a 7x increase in 
   the Mechanical Coherance Length (Lc). This indicates that the DNA backbone 
   may facilitate mechanical signal transduction over several helical turns.

2. Spectral Heterogeneity Patterns:
   Regulatory sites appear to exhibit a 3.7x increase in Spectral Entropy 
   Varience compared to structural controls. This suggests that biological 
   "function" may correlate with specific patterns of spectral variability.

3. Axial Spectral Singularities:
   Using a 3-sigma deviation protocol (Delta_ηr > 0.13), we have identified 
   rare configurations, termed "Axial Snaps," which appear to be high-energy 
   transition states (e.g., PDB 139D, Snap: 0.818).

4. Genomic Census (N=5,075):
   Our automated analysis of the PDB hard dataset suggests that spectral moment 
   invariants provide a reliable basis for topological categorization.
