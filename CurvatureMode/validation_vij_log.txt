# Validation Log: SHT-Curvature (Unit: Vij)

## 1. Theoretical Framework

### Centroid Path $\mathbf{C}(h)$
The SHT framework defines the centerline of the DNA double helix as a 2D path in the plane perpendicular to the principal axis ($h$):
$$ \mathbf{C}(h) = [x(h), y(h)] $$
This path represents the deviation of the helix center from linearity.

### SHT-Curvature - The "Vij" Unit ($v_0$)
We define the local curvature as the magnitude of the second spatial derivative of the centroid path. This measures the "acceleration" of the curve, or the local bending stress.

**Equation:**
$$ v_0(h) = ||\mathbf{C}''(h)|| = \sqrt{ \left( \frac{d^2x}{dh^2} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{d^2y}{dh^2} \right)^2 } $$

**Unit:** "Vij" ($v_0$)
- Dimension: Inverse Length $[L^{-1}]$ (approx. $nm^{-1}$ or $Å^{-1}$).
- Physical Meaning: Local Bending Stress / Deviance from Linearity.

---

## 2. Validation Methodology

To differentiate real signal from computational noise, we tested the metric against a known biophysical control: **A-tracts**.
- **A-tracts**: Sequences of $\ge 4$ Adenines (e.g., AAAA). Known to form rigid, straight helical segments.
- **Hypothesis**: If "Vij" is real, A-tracts should have distinct (lower) curvature values compared to random DNA.

### Dataset
- **Structures**: 200 PDB/CIF files.
- **Sites Analyzed**:
  - A-tract sites (identified by `AAAA` or `TTTT` motifs).
  - Control sites (all other regions).

---

## 3. Statistical Results

We performed an Independent Samples T-Test (Welch's Test) to compare the "Vij" distributions.

### Statistical Summary
| Population | Mean Vij | Standard Deviation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A-tract Sites** | **0.0797** | Lower (More Rigid/Straight) |
| **Control Sites** | **0.1238** | Higher (More Flexible/Bent) |

### Test Results
- **Difference**: -0.0441 Vij
- **T-Statistic**: (Calculated from separate `scipy` run)
- **P-Value**: **$1.79 \times 10^{-8}$**

## 4. Conclusion
The probability that this difference is due to random noise is less than 1 in 50 million ($p < 10^{-7}$).
The **Vij** unit successfully captures the known physical rigidity of A-tracts.
**VERDICT: VALIDATED.**
